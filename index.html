I’m currently pursuing an M.Sc. in Computer Science at Shahid Beheshti University, where I spend most of my time obsessing over elegant algorithms, efficient learning systems, and the art of squeezing meaning out of high-dimensional chaos.

My academic and research journey is driven by a deep love for intelligent systems—especially those that blend mathematical rigor with practical elegance. I’m mostly interested in problems that sit at the intersection of machine learning, advanced algorithm design, and performance-aware systems engineering. I believe great models aren't just accurate—they're fast, interpretable, and built with care.

My current thesis—“Enhancing Latent Data Representation to Overcome Challenges in Single-Cell RNA Sequence Analysis for Improved Cell Type Classification Accuracy”—explores a supervised variational autoencoder tailored for messy, noisy, and delightfully complex biological data. The goal? Build better embeddings that respect the underlying biology while achieving high classification performance.

I love working from first principles—reverse-engineering ideas, reconstructing models from scratch, and diving into research papers like puzzles waiting to be solved. Whether it's tweaking a loss function or dissecting a matrix operation, I enjoy knowing before doing.
